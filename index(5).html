Best places to visit in Japan

Kyoto

Travelers most intrested in Japan's history and traditions should head to kyoto. Centrally located on the archipelago, Kyoto has long been considered the cultural capital of Japan.Here, you will find more than 1,000 Buddhist temples and 400-plus Shinto shrines,including a whopping 17 UNESCO World Heritages sites.

Tokyo

This metropolis is a feast for the senses.Neighborhood like Ginza and Akihabara buzz with flashing ligths and larger-than-life shopping, while Meiji Shrine and the Imeperial Palace give you a look into Japan's storied past.Tokyo is regularly regarded as a top foodie city .

Mount Fuji

The country's tallest mountain and one of its most iconic symbols is a popular destination for outdoor recreation.For centuries,Japanese artists and poets have been inspired by Mount Fuji's alomst perfectly round form.The Fuji Five Lakes region at the foot of this UNESCO World Heritage Site makes a great bese for the thousands of climbers who visit each year.Enjoy the area's museums and amusement park during the warmer months. Or, arrive in winter to soak in the hot springs and ski Mount Fuji's slopes.

Delicious Japanese Cuisine that Can Blow Your Mind

Sushi

Across the nation, the japanese are primarily fish eaters.Japan is the number one fish importer in the world, consumming around 12% of the world's caught fish. The most well-known Japanese dish is probably sushi, a dish that includes fresh fish,seaweed and lightly-seasoned rice. Japanese also eat beef,poultry and pork as part of their daily diets

Japanese Traditional Dress

Japanese dress

The traditional dress of Japan is the kimono.Kimono, which are generally made of silk, have large sleeves and reach from the shoulders all the way down to the heels.They are tied with a wide belt called an obi.Kimonos are now usually worm only on special occas ions, such as the Shinchi-Go-San festival,weddings and graduation ceremonies

Historical Castles

Castle

In Japan there are over one hundred castles.Osaka Castle is one of the Japan's most famous landmarks.As with many traditional Japanese castles, the Osaka Castle is built on a man-made stonewall; it is eight stories high and surrounded by a moat.the castle was erected on the site of a temple and former imperial place.
